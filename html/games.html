Accessibility Challenge Games

Explore the Simulations

Experience interactive simulations that reveal how accessibility barriers affect real users in digital environments.

Each challenge represents a different type of impairment—visual, motor, auditory, or perceptual—to help you understand how design choices impact usability.

Why this matters

Digital environments are not experienced the same way by everyone. Over 1 billion people globally live with some form of disability that affects how they interact with visual, auditory, or motor-based systems.

These simulations are designed to help you understand how design choices in games and applications can either support or exclude users. Each challenge represents a simplified model of real-world impairments—not to replicate lived experience, but to build awareness and better design thinking.

Before You Play

These games are short awareness activities. They are not medical simulations and do not fully represent lived experiences of disability. Instead, they show how common design choices can make games harder to use.

Look for barriers

Notice when information becomes harder to see, hear, control, or understand.

Think like a designer

Consider what accessibility feature could reduce the barrier.

Reflect after playing

Each challenge links back to real accessibility features used in inclusive design.

Core Accessibility Principles

These simulations are based on established accessibility design principles used in real-world digital products:

  • 👁️Perceivable: Information must be available through multiple senses.
  • ⌨️Operable: Interfaces must support different physical abilities.
  • 💡Understandable: Interactions should be clear and predictable.
  • 🧩Robust: Content should work across assistive technologies.

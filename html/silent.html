What this simulation represents

This simulation represents hearing-related accessibility barriers in games. It shows how difficult gameplay can become when important warnings are only communicated through sound.

Real-world impact

Deaf and hard-of-hearing players may miss audio-based warnings, dialogue, enemy direction, timers, or feedback if no visual alternative is included. This can affect reaction time, confidence, and overall gameplay experience.

Design takeaway

Good accessibility design uses multiple forms of feedback. Subtitles, captions, visual alerts, icons, vibration cues, and directional indicators can help make games more inclusive.
